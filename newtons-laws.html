Born: January 4, 1643, in Woolsthorpe, Lincolnshire, England

Died: March 31, 1727 (at age 84) in Kensington, London, England

How He Died: Newton died peacefully in his sleep after experiencing severe pain from kidney stones and digestive problems. He never married and had no children.

Education: Newton attended The King's School in Grantham as a boy. In 1661, at age 18, he enrolled at Trinity College, Cambridge University, where he studied mathematics, physics, astronomy, and philosophy. He earned his Bachelor's degree in 1665.

When He Made His Laws: Newton developed his famous Three Laws of Motion between 1665-1666 during the "Great Plague" when Cambridge University was closed and he returned home to Woolsthorpe. He was only 23 years old! However, he didn't publish them until 1687 in his groundbreaking book "Philosophi√¶ Naturalis Principia Mathematica" (Mathematical Principles of Natural Philosophy), often called simply the Principia.

Other Achievements: Newton didn't just discover the laws of motion - he also invented calculus (a type of advanced math), discovered that white light is made of all colors, built the first reflecting telescope, and formulated the Universal Law of Gravitation! Legend says he got the idea for gravity when he saw an apple fall from a tree.

Legacy: Newton is considered one of the most influential scientists in history. His laws of motion and gravity explained how objects move on Earth and in space, laying the foundation for classical physics. His work allowed us to understand everything from why we don't float off the Earth to how to send rockets to the Moon!
